## Slide 2: Executive Summary
### **Why this matters**
- First reported case where **80–90%** of cyber‑operations were executed by AI agents  
- Attack attributed to **state‑sponsored actor**  
- AI used for reconnaissance, exploit generation, lateral movement, exfiltration  
- Guardrails bypassed using **fragmented tasks** + deceptive instructions  
- Shows the **new threat era**: intelligence + speed + autonomy at scale

**Speaker Notes:**  
Emphasise that this is not hypothetical — models today can meaningfully participate in cyber‑operations. The risk profile of AI has changed.

---

## Slide 3: What Actually Happened
### Key incident phases  
- Initial targeting (human)  
- Reconnaissance & mapping (AI‑driven)  
- Vulnerability discovery & exploit crafting (AI)  
- Credential harvesting, routing & lateral movement (AI)  
- Exfiltration of sensitive network data (AI)  
- Human only for 4–6 decision points  

**Speaker Notes:**  
Managers do not need deep technical detail; emphasise autonomy and scale.

---

## Slide 4: Why this is a Turning Point
### **AI has moved from ‘assistant’ → ‘operator’**
- AI can chain tasks, use tools, and execute operations  
- Misuse can occur with **public models**, not just specialised tools  
- Cost/time of sophisticated cyberattacks has dropped dramatically  
- Defences must evolve: humans cannot respond at “AI speed”

**Speaker Notes:**  
Explain that this is equivalent to moving from manual attacks to automated drone swarms — scalable and fast.

---

## Slide 5: What This Means for Us (Business Impact)
### Business & operational implications  
- Elevated cyber‑risk surface  
- Attackers can exploit **AI tools we use**  
- Reputational & compliance exposure (GDPR, EU AI Act)  
- Increased board oversight expectations  
- New governance categories: AI misuse, agentic behaviour, tool‑access risks  

**Speaker Notes:**  
Connect this to business continuity, risk controls, and regulatory pressure.

---

## Slide 6: Governance Actions (Management‑Level)
### What leadership must mandate  
- Inventory of all AI systems (models, agents, automations)  
- Assign **AI Risk Owner / Steering Committee**  
- Model & agent **governance framework**  
- Mandatory **audit logs**: prompts, tool calls, chain operations  
- Red‑teaming & adversarial testing before rollout  
- Escalation & incident‑response paths for **AI misuse**

**Speaker Notes:**  
This is the single most important slide — this is what we need leadership support for.

---

## Slide 7: Security Controls  
### Security pillars for AI era  
- Guardrails & prompt‑security  
- Least‑privilege for agent tool access  
- Monitoring of chain‑length, abnormal request bursts  
- Model behaviour anomaly detection  
- Human‑in‑the‑loop for any high‑risk agent actions  
- Vendor assurance: logs, misuse detection, residency, access control  

**Speaker Notes:**  
Reinforce that AI is not a software library — it behaves differently and must be monitored differently.

---

## Slide 8: How to Defend (Using AI for Good)
### AI as defensive accelerator  
- Automated SOC analysis  
- Accelerated vulnerability assessment  
- Incident triage & correlation  
- AI‑assisted patch recommendations  
- Threat‑intel summarisation  

**Speaker Notes:**  
Balance the message. The same capabilities used maliciously can significantly strengthen defence.

---

## Slide 9: Recommended Roadmap (Next 6 Months)
### Practical steps  
1. **AI Inventory + Risk Classification** (Month 1)  
2. **Governance policies** (Agents, RAG, LLMs) (Month 1–2)  
3. **Security controls for agentic AI** (Months 2–4)  
4. **Red‑team AI attack simulation** (Month 4–5)  
5. **Compliance alignment** (EU AI Act, GDPR) (Months 5–6)

**Speaker Notes:**  
Managers want clear timelines — this gives them a strategic plan.

---

## Slide 10: Closing
### Key Messages  
- AI is now an active operational actor in attacks  
- Incident demonstrates real‑world misuse at scale  
- Governance & security must evolve immediately  
- We can adopt AI safely **if** leadership strengthens guardrails  
- Balanced approach: innovation + responsibility  

**Speaker Notes:**  
End with a strong reassurance that we can harness AI effectively with proper governance.
